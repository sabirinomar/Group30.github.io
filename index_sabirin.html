Is San Francisco Safe at Night?

San Francisco is known for its vibrant nightlife, attracting both locals and tourists to its bars, restaurants, and cultural hotspots. But how safe is the city after dark?

This project explores crime patterns during nighttime hours in San Francisco, focusing on three key crime types: assault, robbery, and motor vehicle theft. These categories were selected because they directly influence how safe people feel at night. Assault and robbery represent immediate personal danger, while vehicle theft reflects risks associated with parking and mobility during nightlife activities.

Key Question: Does crime increase during nightlife hours, and how does this affect perceived safety?

What Happens at Night?

To understand nightlife safety, we focus on crimes occurring during evening and late-night hours. It is important to state that the data is based on 2003-2026 crime reports, which may not capture all incidents but provides a comprehensive overview of trends over time. The chart below shows how crime levels vary across the week, normalized to highlight patterns rather than absolute numbers.

All three crime types show a clear pattern: incidents increase towards the weekend and peak on Saturday and Sunday. This aligns with increased nightlife activity. Violent crimes such as assault and robbery rise significantly during these periods, suggesting higher risks in crowded social environments. At the same time, motor vehicle theft also peaks, indicating that even when individuals are not directly targeted, their property may still be vulnerable.
